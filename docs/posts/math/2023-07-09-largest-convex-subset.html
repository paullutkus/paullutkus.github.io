Largest Convex Subset

Last updated: July 10, 2023
Tags: Geometry, Convexity

Introduction

Consider a closed, nonconvex figure in the plane. What is its largest convex subset? This does seem like a well founded question, and the problem as a whole can be used as a model for exploration as I’ll discuss.

Figure 1 illustrates the question at hand. The amoeba shape is nonconvex set in question, and the shaded triangle is a guess at the largest convex subset. Don’t look to closely though, its easy to pick out ways in which the triangle estimation can be improved.
